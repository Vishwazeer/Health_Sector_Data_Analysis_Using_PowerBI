# Healthcare Data Analysis using Power BI

---

**Introduction**

Built an interactive Power BI dashboard to uncover key trends in hospital visitor data, enabling smarter, data-driven decision-making. Leveraged DAX to gain deeper insights into patient demographics and service usage, helping improve operational efficiency and patient satisfaction. The dashboard integrates multiple data sources, applies data modeling principles, and uses Power Query for seamless data transformation. With built-in KPIs and dynamic visualizations, healthcare professionals can better optimize resources and elevate the quality of patient care.

**Features**

#### Data Understanding:

The healthcare dataset contains fields such as Date, ID, Gender, Age, Race, Moment (AM/PM), Weekday/Weekend, Admin Flag (Patient/Non-Patient), Department Referral, and Satisfaction Score. Together, these attributes provide a comprehensive view of visitor demographics, visit patterns, and department engagement — forming a solid foundation for trend analysis and operational insights.

---

#### Data Preprocessing:

- **Data Normalization and Imputation:** The dataset was processed through an ETL (Extract, Transform, Load) pipeline within Power Query Editor. This involved normalizing the data by splitting tables for better organization and clarity, while missing values were handled using imputation techniques to ensure consistency and reliability across all features.

- **DAX Calculations and Data Modeling:** DAX (Data Analysis Expressions) was used to create calculated fields and aggregations, enabling in-depth analysis of the healthcare data. Relationships between tables were established through data modeling, resulting in richer insights and a more interpretable dashboard.

---

#### Visit Trends and Patterns:

- **Daily Visits:** Daily visitor counts tend to rise gradually throughout the month, peaking near the end before dropping on the final day — suggesting a surge in hospital traffic during the last week of each month.

- **Monthly Visits:** Visitor numbers notably increased between April and October, aligning with summer and rainy seasons. This pattern highlights the significant role seasonal factors play in driving hospital traffic.

- **Yearly Visits:** Visitor counts grew by **5.8%** from 2019 to 2020, potentially reflecting a rise in health concerns or growing confidence in the hospital's services — pointing to both higher healthcare demand and improved patient satisfaction.

- **Quarterly Visits:** The second and third quarters recorded visitor volumes **53.9% higher** than the first and fourth quarters, indicating that mid-year periods experience peak traffic, likely driven by seasonal illness trends.

---

#### Time-Based Distribution:

- **Moment Distribution:** Visitor flow is nearly equal between AM and PM hours, with AM visits slightly higher by **0.6%** — reflecting consistent demand throughout the day from both scheduled appointments and walk-ins.

- **Day of Week Distribution:** Mondays and Wednesdays see the highest visitor volumes, while Fridays record the lowest — suggesting a mid-week preference for hospital visits, possibly tied to increased medical needs early in the week.

- **Weekday vs. Weekend:** Weekday visits are **148.83% higher** than weekend visits, a significant gap likely driven by reduced staffing or limited hours on weekends, which naturally pushes patients to visit during the week.

---

#### Visitor Wait Time Analysis:

- **Average Wait Time:** The average wait time is approximately **35 minutes**, with **90.9%** of visitors waiting between 20 to 60 minutes — highlighting a clear opportunity to streamline processes and reduce wait times.

- **Short Wait Times:** Only **9.1%** of visitors experience wait times in the 10 to 20-minute range, underscoring the need for improved service efficiency and better overall patient experience.

---

#### Demographic Insights:

- **Age Group Distribution:** Visitors across age groups from 0 to 75 show fairly consistent visit frequencies, while the 75+ age group makes up just **5.04%** of total visitors — possibly reflecting a smaller demographic or fewer healthcare visits in that range.

- **Race Distribution:** White and African American visitors form the majority of the patient population, with Asian visitors at moderate levels. Pacific Islander and Native American groups together account for **11.37%**, reflecting a diverse and multicultural patient base.

- **Gender Distribution:** Male visitors outnumber female visitors by **4.86%**, while those identifying as "Not Specified" represent a minimal **0.26%** — a slight disparity that points to the value of targeted outreach and engagement strategies.

---

#### Satisfaction and Departmental Insights:

- **Satisfaction Score Distribution:** The average satisfaction score stands at **5 out of 10**, suggesting that most visitors have a neutral perception of the services received — indicating meaningful room for quality improvement.

- **Department Referrals:** General Practice and Orthopedics together account for **30.75%** of referrals, while departments like Physiotherapy and Cardiology represent **10.64%**. Notably, non-referred patients make up a substantial **58.67%** of all visitors.

- **Admin Flag Distribution:** **50.04%** of visitors are fully registered patients, while the remaining **49.96%** are non-registered individuals or general visitors — indicating that nearly half of the hospital's traffic comes from people not enrolled for formal treatment.
